Stay Safe Online - Prevention Checklist

General Safety Tips

1. Use strong and different passwords for each account. It might feel extra work, but it keeps you much safer. Turning on 2FA (two-factor authentication) adds an extra layer of protection.

2. Don't click on unknown links or download random files, even if they look urgent or interesting.

3. Always double-check who is contacting you before replying to emails, messages, or calls. Scammers often pretend to be trusted people or companies.

4. Avoid logging into important accounts on public Wi-Fi, especially for banking or personal use. It's not always secure.

5. Keep your phone and apps updated, as updates fix security issues and protect your data. Think before sharing personal information online, especially on social media. Not

everything needs to be public.

Financial / UPI Safety Tips

1. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or bank details with anyone - no genuine bank will ever ask for it.

2. Remember: If someone is sending you money, you don't need to enter your PIN or approve anything. That's a common scam trick.

3. Always read payment requests carefully before accepting them. One small mistake can cost money.

4. Use only trusted and official payment apps to avoid fraud.

Reporting & Action

1. If something feels wrong, act quickly.

2. Report cyber fraud on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in

3. Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 for immediate help in financial fraud cases.

4. Also, inform your bank immediately if you notice any suspicious transaction.

Final Thought

Staying safe online is mostly about being aware and careful. A few simple habits can save you from big problems.
